The 60-Second Version (with proof)

You built a design system. Every token, every component, every variable — intentional. Then AI tools come along and generate frames that look right but fall apart on inspection. No components, no tokens, no auto-layout. The cleanup takes as long as building it yourself.

Mimic doesn't approximate your design system. It uses it.

Give it any HTML — a prototype, a competitor page, a wireframe — and it builds the equivalent in Figma using your real components, your real tokens, your real text styles. Auto-layout everywhere. Not blue rectangles.

After every build, it produces a report: what components it used, what it built from primitives and why, and what your DS is missing — with concrete recommendations. Correct a mapping once and it remembers forever. By the tenth build, most decisions are instant. Each build costs less than the last, and your DS keeps improving as a side effect of using it.

And without any extra effort from you, it writes component descriptions from real usage and generates a DESIGN.md for your system. Figma Make, Stitch, generative UI — they all need component metadata to work properly. Mimic produces it from your actual builds, not from a manual audit.

It's open source. Runs locally. Your design data never leaves your machine.

Variations by Audience

To a designer who maintains a DS

Give Mimic any HTML and it builds the Figma equivalent using your actual components and tokens. After every build it tells you what's missing from your DS with concrete recommendations. It learns from your corrections and gets better every time — less cleanup, less cost, and your DS improves just by using it. It also writes the component descriptions that Figma Make and Stitch need to use your system properly.

To a developer evaluating MCP tools

Mimic is an MCP server that translates HTML into Figma using the user's design system. 45 tools, strict DS enforcement, learning loop that caches component mappings across builds. Post-build reports with DS gap analysis and efficiency metrics. Generates DESIGN.md in the open format. Runs locally, MIT license, zero telemetry.

To a design system lead / design ops

Every time Mimic builds a screen, it produces a report: which components it used, which are missing, and what to add. It learns from every build — fewer primitives, fewer calls, better compliance. Over time your DS improves as a side effect of building with it. And without any manual work, it generates the component metadata that Figma Make, Stitch, and generative UI need to actually use your system.

To a friend in 10 seconds

It converts HTML to Figma using your actual design system — real components, real tokens. It gets smarter every time, and it prepares your DS for all the AI tools that are coming.

What NOT to say

  • "AI-powered Figma plugin" — vague, sounds like 50 other tools. Mimic is an MCP server, not a plugin.
  • "Automates design" — designers hear "replaces me." Mimic is a reviewer and copilot, not an automation tool.
  • "Revolutionary" / "game-changing" — instant credibility loss. Let the feature speak.
  • Technical details first — nobody cares about MCP, bridge architecture, or tool counts until they understand the value.
  • "Works with any design system" — technically true (N=2 tested) but sounds like a claim you can't back yet. Say "works with your design system" instead.
